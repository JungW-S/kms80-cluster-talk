Cluster algebras were introduced by Fomin and Zelevinsky, motivated by canonical bases and total positivity.

They now appear in many areas, including Lie theory, representation theory, and algebraic geometry.

In this talk, we compare two cluster algebra structures from different origins.

Definition

The bosonic extension \(\widehat{\calA}=\widehat{\calA}(\mathfrak g)\) is the \(\mathbb Q(q^{1/2})\)-algebra with generators

\[ \{f_{i,p}\mid (i,p)\in I\times\mathbb Z\}, \]

subject to the quantum Serre relations \[ \sum_{t=0}^{1-c_{i,j}} (-1)^t \begin{bmatrix}1-c_{i,j}\\ t\end{bmatrix}_{q} f_{i,p}^{\,1-c_{i,j}-t}f_{j,p}f_{i,p}^{\,t}=0 \quad (i\ne j). \] and the \(q\)-Boson relation. \(f_{i,m}f_{j,p} = q^{(-1)^{p-m+1}(\alpha_i,\alpha_j)} f_{j,p}f_{i,m}\) \({}+\delta_{i,j}\delta_{p,m+1}(1-q^2), \quad\text{if }m<p.\)

Setup
\[ \Delta\in\Br^{+}: \text{ the half twist, i.e. the lift of }w_\circ, \qquad \rxw\in\Seq(\Delta). \]
\[ \uii=(i_1,\ldots,i_r)\in\Seq(\ttb). \]
\[ \text{We consider the braid variety }X(\rxw\uii). \]

This braid variety is isomorphic to the half-decorated double Bott-Samelson variety \(\Conf(\uii)\).

For an admissible chain \(\frakC=(\frakc_t)_{t=1}^r\) of \(\uii\), we construct a Demazure weave

\[ \fW_{\Delta}(\frakC). \]
Theorem (Huh-J.-Kim-Park)

There is a unique algebra isomorphism

\[ \varphi_{\uii}:\tobbA_{\C}(\ttb) \xrightarrow{\sim} \C[X(\rxw\uii)], \qquad \varphi_{\uii}(\bFF_{\uii,t})=z_t \quad(1\le t\le r). \]

Seed comparison:

\[ \begin{aligned} \varphi_{\uii}\bigl(\mD(\frakc_t)\bigr) &= A_t\bigl(\fW_{\Delta}(\frakC)\bigr) \qquad(1\le t\le r),\\ Q\bigl(\fW_{\Delta}(\frakC)\bigr) &= Q(\frakC)^{\rm op}. \end{aligned} \]

Remark. This algebra isomorphism was first observed by Fan Qin.

  • Let \(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}}\) be an affine Kac-Moody algebra.
  • There is a way to associate a finite ADE type \(\g\) to \(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}}\).
  • The positive braid \(\ttb\) gives a subcategory \[ \mathscr C_{\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}}}^{\mathbb D}(\ttb) \] of finite-dimensional \(U_q'(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}})\)-modules.
  • This subcategory contains \(C_t^{\mathbb D,\uii}\) affine cuspidal modules and \(M^{\mathbb D,\uii}[a,b]\) affine determinantial modules.
Theorem (Kashiwara-Kim-Oh-Park)
\[ K\bigl(\mathscr C_{\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}}}^{\mathbb D}(\ttb)\bigr) \xrightarrow{\sim}\obbA(\ttb) \] \[ [C_t^{\mathbb D,\uii}]\longmapsto \bFF_{\uii,t}, \qquad [M^{\mathbb D,\uii}[a,b]]\longmapsto \mD_{\uii}[a,b]. \]
Corollary (Huh-J.-Kim-Park)

Assume \(\mathfrak g_{\mathrm{aff}}\) is of type \(A_n^{(1)}\). For any \(i\)-box \([a,b]\) of \(\uii\),

\[ [M^{\mathbb D,\uii}[a,b]] = \triangle_{i_a} \bigl( B_{i_a}([C_a^{\mathbb D,\uii}]) B_{i_{a+1}}([C_{a+1}^{\mathbb D,\uii}]) \cdots B_{i_b}([C_b^{\mathbb D,\uii}]) \bigr). \] \[ B_i(z)= \operatorname{diag}\left( I_{i-1}, \begin{pmatrix}z&-1\\ 1&0\end{pmatrix}, I_{n-i} \right), \qquad \triangle_i=\text{the leading principal }i\times i\text{ minor}. \]
Example

In type \(A_3^{(1)}\), let \(\uii=(2,3,1,2,2,1)\), and write \(C_t:=[C_t^{\mathbb D,\uii}]\). For the \(i\)-box \([3,6]\), the corollary gives

\[ [M^{\mathbb D,\uii}[3,6]] = \triangle_1\bigl(B_1(C_3)B_2(C_4)B_2(C_5)B_1(C_6)\bigr). \] \[ B_1(C_3)B_2(C_4)B_2(C_5)B_1(C_6) = \begin{pmatrix} C_3C_6-C_4C_5+1&-C_3&C_4&0\\ C_6&-1&0&0\\ C_5&0&-1&0\\ 0&0&0&1 \end{pmatrix}. \] \[ [M^{\mathbb D,\uii}[3,6]]=C_3C_6-C_4C_5+1. \]
